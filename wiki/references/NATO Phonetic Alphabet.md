> [!key-insight] The NATO/ICAO spelling alphabet removes ambiguity when reading
> characters aloud — license keys, serial numbers, MAC addresses, IPs,
> confirmation codes — over a phone or radio where **B/P/D/T/V**, **M/N**, and
> **S/F** are easily misheard. Say the word, not the letter.

## Letters

| Letter | Word | Letter | Word |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| A | Alpha | N | November |
| B | Bravo | O | Oscar |
| C | Charlie | P | Papa |
| D | Delta | Q | Quebec |
| E | Echo | R | Romeo |
| F | Foxtrot | S | Sierra |
| G | Golf | T | Tango |
| H | Hotel | U | Uniform |
| I | India | V | Victor |
| J | Juliet | W | Whiskey |
| K | Kilo | X | X-ray |
| L | Lima | Y | Yankee |
| M | Mike | Z | Zulu |

## Digits

Read digits individually. Aviation/radio convention pronounces a few oddly to
avoid confusion:

| Digit | Say | | Digit | Say |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 0 | Zero | | 5 | Five |
| 1 | One | | 6 | Six |
| 2 | Two | | 7 | Seven |
| 3 | Three (*"tree"*) | | 8 | Eight |
| 4 | Four | | 9 | Niner |

## Usage

- **Confirm case + symbols explicitly:** "capital Alpha", "lowercase bravo",
  "dash", "underscore", "dot". Spelling alphabets only cover the letter — the
  caller still has to hear `A` vs `a`.
- **Group long strings** ("Alpha-Bravo-Charlie — break — One-Two-Three") so the
  other side can read back a chunk at a time.
- Handy when reading out the values in [[Networking Reference]] (IPs, MACs) or
  any serial / key over the phone.
